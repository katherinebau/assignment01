Camp Kesem

This summer, I spent my last week at Camp Kesem. Camp Kesem is a camp for kids whose parents have cancer. Its a haven for them to go where others understand how they feel and the positions that they are in. At Camp Kesem, I met the sunniest, more inspiring, more resilient campers. All with big hearts even in the face of difficult times. All still willing to comfort others and to feel their pain. I learned more than I thought I ever could from those campers.

Things I learned at Camp Kesem
  • Gaga ball is super intense
  • Friendship bracelets are a favorite for tween girls and myself
  • Everyone has the capacity to throw kindness up in the air
  • It's hard to be an ugly crier at this camp - but it's also more than okay
  • Scrapes are okay if there/s a friend with you
